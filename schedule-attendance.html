Parent schedule + attendance · trust at the money gate
C2 of the customer-app redesign. The parent sees upcoming classes and approves a completed class, the tap that pays the tutor and bills the next month. This is not a tap problem; it is a clarity and trust problem. For Hafiz · 25 June 2026.
The Approve tap is the business engine: it releases the tutor's pay and mints the next invoice. Yet today the parent taps Approve, confirms, and only then hits a raw error wall if the invoice is unpaid, and they approve money having never seen what was taught. The fixes use data that already exists in the backend: surface the invoice + amount before the tap, show the tutor's session note at the gate, unify the three inconsistent class views and relabel "attended" to "Needs your approval", add a next-class focal card, and make dispute a structured in-app flow.

Commercial pass. Founder: the approve tap is the revenue engine, so de-risking it protects revenue directly. Sales: the pay-gate is a conversion moment (surfaced well, on-time payment rises), and post-approve is the warmest cross-sell. Marketing: the session note is a trust and shareable asset. Guard. The cross-sell is dismissible and never blocks the flow; and Approve must not become so frictionless that the proof review is skipped. Trust at the gate holds.
