Parent request flow · fewer taps, smarter defaults
C1 of the customer-app redesign, after the full reassess. The funnel a parent walks to request a tutor. North Star: get a verified, fitting tutor matched fast, so more requests are completed and the parent keeps paying. For Hafiz · 25 June 2026.
The funnel is honest: every field feeds the price, the matching, the schedule, or the address. So this is a tap cut, not a field cut. After the reassess, the online path goes from 5 screens to 2 and about 19 taps to about 11: the student step folds into screen 1, the separate Review screen folds into the schedule screen, the success screen becomes a toast, and the two request-detail screens become one. A returning parent re-requesting for the same child drops to about 3 taps once the backend exposes "repeat my last request" (the parallel Hafiz ask).

Same request, three paths. The win for new parents is zero-backend; the win for returning parents needs one small backend addition.

Why it matters. Returning parents are the retention core. A re-request that takes 3 taps instead of 19 removes the friction from the most valuable, highest-frequency case.

What it needs. The list endpoint already returns the request rows; it needs the schedule and preference fields included, or a "duplicate this request" action that pre-loads the funnel. No new data, just re-reading what the parent submitted.

Cross-sell (founder direction): a suggested extra subject is pre-selected, but itemised in the price and one-tap removable. Transparent, not a sneaky add (the guard).

Tap-count: open "Request again", Use these details, Submit = about 3 taps.

Cross-sell: a reusable "add a subject" banner
Founder direction: suggest an additional subject at warm moments to lift subjects per parent. On "Request again?" the suggestion is pre-selected (above); the same banner drops into other surfaces. Always a clearly-removable suggestion, neutral copy, never a sneaky add.

What the reassess changed (Step 2.5, founder-approved)
Applied: (1) fold the student step into screen 1 and make "Where" in-person-only, so the online funnel is 2 screens; (2) merge the Review screen into the schedule screen as an inline price; (3) collapse the success screen into a toast onto the request detail; (4) unify the open and completed detail screens into one state-adaptive screen, leading with status and tucking the full recap behind "View full details".
Kept on purpose (the guardrail): "What + who" and "When + confirm" stay separate. Cramming the whole funnel onto one screen crosses two mental models and over one and a half scrolls. Right-sized, not just minimised.
Notes for Hafiz

Zero-backend wins ship now: the 2-screen funnel, surfaced defaults, gender default, the success toast, the unified detail, and the reframed tally. None of these need backend.
