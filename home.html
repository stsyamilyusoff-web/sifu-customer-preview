The balance: warm, and firm where it must be
Two design pillars, held together: the felt value of the spend, and the non-negotiable of getting paid.
Supported · trusted · updated
Home opens on the child's journey, not a promo. A progress snapshot ("Adam is doing well, Understanding 6 to 8, on track"), the next class with the verified tutor, and a fresh report surfaced the moment it lands. The parent feels their investment working and stays in the loop. This is the felt value of spending thousands on their child.
Operationally firm (overdue)
Overdue payments are flagged clearly and unmissably (the founder's non-negotiable), but framed around continuity ("clear it to keep Adam's classes running"), tying to the real consequence (an unpaid invoice blocks the next class), never shaming. Firm, respectful, and honest. The platform gets paid; the parent keeps their dignity.
What this is (Step 2.5 reassess)
Applied: a journey-first Home; a "what needs you" zone with the overdue flag (firm + supportive), the approve money-gate, and the new-report miniature (the biggest gap filled); repointed spokes (C1 matching-status when there is an open request, C3 the explained money block); marketing demoted below the fold. The orphaned `HomeStatsRow` is revived as the journey/triage layer.
Kept on purpose (the guardrail): Home stays a launchpad, not a second copy of each surface. The journey snapshot and the "needs you" items LINK to the full screens (the schedule, the bill, the report); they do not reproduce them. Calm and prioritised, never an anxiety pile of red items. One firm overdue flag, not a wall of them.
