Profile + account hub · five screens, one skin, honest pause
Cluster B of the secondary sweep, expanded. The 5th-tab hub, the parent profile, the students list, add-a-child, and the photo camera, each its own before and after, brought onto the parent design language that already lives in the app's code, with the real broken flows fixed. For Hafiz · 26 June 2026.
The parent skin already exists in code (the design tokens plus the redesigned Invoice / Requests / Profile screens). These five account screens are still on the old look, and three of them hide real bugs: a "Deactivate" row that secretly deletes the account for good, a students list that mislabels gender as "Pref. Tutor" and has no way to add a child, and a profile save that asks for the camera permission to open the photo library and writes hard-coded junk into the database. Each screen below is its own before and after pair, and the after applies the consistency floor (tokens, brand blue, "Sifututor", neutral copy, real success toasts, real empty states).
1 · ProfileMenu (the 5th-tab hub)
The account home. Today it is a long flat list of plain text rows under five headings, with no chevrons and no Settings, and the red "Deactivate / Disable" at the bottom actually performs a permanent account delete (it posts status: resigned and signs you out for good). The redesign groups the rows into inset cards with proper rows, elevates the identity card, and replaces the misleading delete with an honest pause that sits apart from a clearly labelled delete.

Two distinct actions, honestly labelled. Pause keeps everything; delete is clearly the destructive one and matches what the code actually does.

Why this matters. A parent who wants a break should not have to delete everything. Today the only exit is a permanent delete dressed up as "Deactivate / Disable", which is misleading and loses the customer for good. The modal even contradicts the row, its title already says "Delete Account" and "cannot be undone", so the label on the row is the lie.

What it needs (Hafiz Priority 10): a real account pause state on the backend (keep the data, suspend billing and matching, allow reactivation). The existing delete (status: resigned) stays as the separate, clearly labelled destructive action.

Commercial: a pause is a retention save. A parent on a break is far more likely to return than one who deleted, and honest labelling protects trust at the most sensitive moment in the relationship. The pause must stay genuinely reversible, never a dark pattern that quietly keeps billing.
