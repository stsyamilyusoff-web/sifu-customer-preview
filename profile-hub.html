3 · An honest pause (not a secret delete)
The "Deactivate / Disable" row performs a permanent delete today. Settings now offers a real pause that keeps the parent's data, with delete clearly separate.

9:41▮▮▮
Account
Pause my account
Take a break. Your children, history, and reports are kept, and you can return anytime.
Delete account
Permanently remove your account and data. This cannot be undone.
Two distinct actions, honestly labelled. Pause keeps everything; delete is clearly the destructive one.

Why this matters. A parent who wants a break should not have to delete everything. Today the only exit is a permanent delete dressed up as "Deactivate", which is misleading and loses the customer for good.

What it needs (Hafiz Priority 10): a real account pause state on the backend (keep the data, suspend billing and matching, allow reactivation). Delete stays as-is.

Commercial: a pause is a retention save; a parent on a break is far more likely to return than one who deleted. Honest labelling also protects trust.
